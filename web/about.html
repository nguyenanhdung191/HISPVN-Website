About DHIS2

HISP can be described as a global research, development and action network around health information systems for the Global South, enabled through north-south-south collaboration. HISP was initiated in South Africa in 1994/95 in 4 pilot districts in and around Cape Town. The first version of DHIS application was developed in tandem with the first unified standards for health data to be collected from across South Africa. The DHIS, data standards, and the approach to change loosely termed as “using information for action” within a decentralized framework may be termed as HISP-approaches to Health Information Systems (HIS). These approaches were implemented in two provinces in 1998, followed by more in the following 2 years leading to it becoming the national standard from 2001. From these beginnings, HISP today has a presence in different ways and to varying extents with mixed results in more than 20 countries spread over Africa and Asia. Further, HISP is now partnering with various other networks such as WAHO (West African Health Organisation and its 15 member countries) and OpenMRS (a medical record system technology development network) to enable the setting up of configuration of “network of networks.”
